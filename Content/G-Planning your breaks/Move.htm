Move

As mentioned before, movement is what will help you get more glucose to your brain and/or help you process too much sugar consumed during break. Getting some exercise during breaks will offset the adverse effects of adding more sedentary work to your schedule. Walking to the kitchen to get some water or make some tea is already a winning move. Doing chores may be a good option as well. Nowadays they often get a bad rep, but they are a great way to get some exercise with a bonus. When you sweep your floor, you move and you end up with a clean floor, a win-win as long as you frame it as an exercise to help you shake off all the sitting, not a multitasking super-efficiency challenge.

Feeling uninspired still? Maybe try some of the 5 minute workouts available on YouTube: in Polish or in English.

The break is longer than 10 minutes? Then you may squeeze in one of these 10 minute low-impact dance cardio trainings: in Polish or in English. Or just dance like no one's watching. (And make sure no one's watching by turning off your camera.)
