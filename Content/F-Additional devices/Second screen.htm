Second screen

If your current job entails a lot of work with a computer, you are probably well aware by now that staring down at a laptop is bad for you. If the machine you’re using is a laptop, connecting it to an external monitor will save you a lot of neck pain and you’ll have the option of using both of your screens when needed. It will certainly come handy for the more practical classes, where you will be learning how to use certain tools. And there are many practical classes planned in the curriculum of these studies. However, if you don’t own a monitor and never needed one, maybe you don’t need to rush to the store to get one. Do you have a TV set? If so, it’s very likely that it could be connected to your computer and serve as a monitor. The same may be true for your tablet or, the worst case scenario, your phone. Or another laptop maybe? Just remember that if the additional device that you’re using is not connected to the machine you’re working on (e.g. you’re using your computer for your own work with the tools, but you’re connecting to Teams with a separate mobile device), you will not be able to share your screen with your instructor to troubleshoot anything that might have gone wrong.
