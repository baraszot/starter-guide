Final project

The summer semester is largely about the final project. Not in the sense that all classes will be devoted to it, but that you will be expected to work on it parallel to the classes. Your work will need to be reviewed not only by the instructor you choose as your supervisor, but also by peer reviewers you will choose among your fellow students. That means you will be expected to be ready for reviews in May (the earlier, the better) so that you can proceed with the defence in June. You should decide on a topic in March and keep yourself busy with writing in April. While that’s going on, you will be learning new things and may get some homework not related to your final project. Thus, if you’re in the position to make decisions about your workload in your regular job, try to keep yourself less busy for the spring months or, if you know more work is coming then, start on your final project early.

Good luck with your work!
