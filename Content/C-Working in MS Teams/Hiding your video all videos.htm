Hiding your video/all videos from your view

As with most small scale meetings, you will generally be expected to turn your web cam on. (If your internet connection or your machine is not fast enough to allow it, it might be a good idea to explain that to the rest of the group. Research has shown that not having your camera on is generally considered disrespectful.)

However, having cameras on does not mean you have to look at a low quality video of yourself for hours on end. Teams allow some options to avoid that.

To only hide your own video from your view:

  1. Open the menu on your video box.
  2. Choose Hide for me.

The video will be minimised and you can easily retrieve it by choosing Unhide for me in the menu. Others participants will continue to see your video.

When the presenter is sharing their presentation and you are finding all videos distracting, you can choose to view the presentation only. In order to enable that option:

  1. Go to View menu on the top panel.
  2. Choose Focus on content.

This will not only hide all videos from your sight, but also increase the size of the shared content. If you want to go back to seeing others, uncheck the Focus on content function in the View menu. Teams will most likely keep the view you were using before activating Focus function checked, so you will be taken back to that mode (Gallery is the default one you start the meetings with).

To hide all incoming videos (i.e. all videos showing other participants; shared presentation will remain visible), but keep your own visible to you:

  1. Go to View menu on the top panel.
  2. Go to More options.
  3. Choose Turn off incoming video.

If you yourself need to do something that may distract other participants (like taking your laptop and going to another room for better Wi-Fi), remember to turn off your camera for that moment.
