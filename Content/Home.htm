Vistula Tech Comm Student Starter Guide

You’ve enrolled to study Technical Communication at Vistula University and good for you! You will learn a lot and you will meet many interesting people, that’s for sure. The downside is that you’ve also signed up for additional hours of sitting in front of your computer and just generally more hours of things to do to what may already be a busy working week. This guide was written with the intent to help you minimise the negative impact of the studies on your general wellbeing. It is assumed that you’ve experienced on-line meetings before, but they may have been different from what’s ahead of you – you were not studying and/or not using MS Teams.

This guide contains:

  • some introductory information on how the classes are run;
  • an overview of MS Teams options that you may find useful during classes;
  • some tips on using your university e-mail and planning your time wisely;
  • some friendly reminders on things you’d better do if you don’t want to end up feeling literally sick and tired by the end of the course.

Learn from the trials and errors of those who came before you. Click through the pages as it best suits you. Hope this helps!

Frequently Asked Questions

Will there be homework?

Yes, see Homework for details.

How much time does it take to prepare the final project?

A lot depends on you – you choose the topic and the tool. However, there are some dates that you can expect as deadlines for certain phases of the work. See Final project for more information on the general timeframe of the final project.

Will I need anything else other than my computer?

Not necessarily, but for your own comfort you may want to use a second screen and a mobile app. See Additional devices for more details.
